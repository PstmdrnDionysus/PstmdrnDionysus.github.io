TIN Matching

The following section details the TIN matching processes available within TIR - Corporate.

Real-Time TIN Check

To perform a real-time TIN check, perform the following steps:

1.   From the Navigation Menu, select Data.

2.   Click Add Record in the upper right-hand corner of the screen.

3.   Select Recipient from the drop-down list.

4.   From the Add Recipient sliding panel, enter a Name and TIN and click Verify Identity.

5.   When you click Verify Identity, a TIN match, DMF, and OFAC check will be triggered. The results of the check will be displayed as Passed, Failed, or Not Verified.

6.   Once you’ve saved the recipient record, an audit trail of the TIN check will appear in the History tab, located at the bottom of the page.

7.   If you’ve altered a value for TIN, TIN Type, or Name, the Verify Identity button will again be enabled.

Bulk TIN Match

To perform a bulk TIN match, perform the following steps:

1.   From the Navigation Menu, select Data.

2.   Click Start Import on the upper right-hand corner of the page to view the Import sliding panel.

3.   Follow the previously detailed import process and perform a Recipient import; for more information on the import process, see {Link}Import.

Note: During the import process, a bulk TIN file is created and queued to be sent to the IRS. Between one and five days after import, depending on the TIN matching volume at the IRS, each recipient record from that import will have the TIN status under Identify Management updated to Passed or Failed.

Identity Management Status

To view all TINs that have been matched with real-time TIN and bulk TIN, perform the following steps:

1.   From the Navigation Menu, select Data.

2.   The Identity Management section displays the following three cards; TIN Mismatches, OFAC Alerts, and DMF Alerts.

3.   Click TIN Mismatches to view a breakdown of the amount of records that have each IRS error code.

a. Click into each row to display a list of records that have that IRS error code.

4.   When you click Verify Identity, the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) check will review the name of a recipient against the OFAC database. A status of Passed represents a Sovos calculated OFAC Score of less than 75, and a status of Failed represents a Sovos calculated OFAC Score of more than 75.

Note: The information above only shows OFAC results that have failed.

5.   When you click Verify Identity, the Death Master File (DMF) check will review the Name and TIN combination against the IRS database. A status of Passed represents a recipient that is not on the DMF list, and a status of Failed represents a recipient that is on the DMF list.
